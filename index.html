Remina Greenfield

I am a New York–based cultural strategist and creative leader working across contemporary art, emerging technologies, and the sciences. My work is driven by a core question: how experimental ideas at the edges of art, science, and technology can be translated into forms that move public audiences. Over the past decade, I have shaped the conceptual direction of complex, cross-disciplinary projects, building the intellectual frameworks, technical infrastructures, and institutional partnerships that allow ambitious work to take root, while maintaining rigorous operational and financial discipline.

As Studio Director and former Director of Research at Anicka Yi Studio, I drive the creative and research vision behind a body of work that spans kelp-based sculptures and autonomous floating machines to paintings and video produced through machine learning. The studio's projects have been presented internationally at Tate Modern, the Venice Biennale, TED, MIT List Visual Arts Center, and UCCA Beijing. Through my curatorial practice, I convene artists, scientists, and technologists around urgent questions of nonhuman intelligence, ecological entanglement, and machinic creativity, developing symposia and exhibitions with partners including Pirelli HangarBicocca, Stanford University, and NEW INC.

I steward seven-figure, multi-stakeholder budgets across international productions, build artist-centered operational systems, and develop ethical cross-sector partnerships. I hold an MFA in Design and Technology from Parsons School of Design, where I have designed and taught graduate and core curriculum courses. A participant in NEW INC’s Year 7 Creative Science Track I remain an active member of that community. My leadership is grounded in equity, sustainable growth, and care.
