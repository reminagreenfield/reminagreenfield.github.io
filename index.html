Remina Greenfield

I’m a New York–based cultural strategist and researcher working at the intersection of contemporary art, emerging technologies, and the sciences. Over the past decade, I have shaped complex, collaborative projects that translate experimental research into compelling public form—building the conceptual, technical, and institutional structures that allow ambitious work to take hold.

I am currently Studio Manager and former Director of Research at Anicka Yi Studio, where I have led research and development for projects ranging from kelp-based sculpture to autonomous floating machines to paintings produced through machine learning. The studio’s work has been presented internationally at institutions including Tate Modern, the Venice Biennale, the MIT List Visual Arts Center, and the Leeum Museum of Art. My curatorial practice includes symposia and exhibitions developed with partners such as Pirelli HangarBicocca, Stanford University, and NEW INC.

Across roles, I steward seven-figure, multi-stakeholder budgets across international productions and institutional timelines; build artist-centered systems; and develop ethical cross-sector partnerships. I hold an MFA in Design and Technology from Parsons School of Design, where I have designed and taught graduate and core curriculum courses as a part-time faculty member. I participated in NEW INC’s Year 7 Creative Science Track and remain an active community member. My work is grounded in equity-driven leadership and sustainable growth rooted in care.
